# Phase 11 - Bivariate Analysis

## Scope

This report compares CSAT with selected operational variables. Results are descriptive associations and do not establish causal effects.

## Summary

| Variable | Groups | Eta-squared | Observed CSAT Pattern |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Response-time band | 8 | 0.04495 | CSAT decreases as response delay increases |
| Sub-category | 57 | 0.03065 | Material variation across issue types |
| Category | 12 | 0.00852 | Cancellation and product/order categories underperform |
| Agent tenure | 5 | 0.00233 | On Job Training has the lowest average |
| Channel | 3 | 0.00228 | Email has the lowest average |
| Shift | 5 | 0.00182 | Morning has the lowest average |

Eta-squared values are modest. Response time and sub-category show the strongest relative separation among the evaluated variables, but most CSAT variation remains unexplained by any single grouping.

## Key Comparisons

- Channel: Email averages 3.8991 versus 4.2514 for Inbound tickets and 4.2699 for Outcall.
- Category: Cancellation averages 3.9905 among categories with at least 100 records. Very small groups show wider values and should not drive conclusions.
- Response time: average CSAT falls from 4.4844 for responses within 5 minutes to 3.3952 for responses over 24 hours.
- Tenure: On Job Training averages 4.1452, below all established tenure bands.
- Shift: Morning averages 4.1895; Split has the highest average at 4.4274.

## Interpretation Limits

Group composition, case difficulty, staffing, and issue mix may confound these comparisons. Further modeling should validate whether the observed relationships remain after controlling for other variables.
